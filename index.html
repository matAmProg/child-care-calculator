Cost of Child Care During the Coronavirus Pandemic

Select your state from the drop-down menu below to populate the calculator with default data.

Next, select if you want to estimate the cost of child care pre-pandemic (Current Scenario), or the cost of child care meeting emergency child care regulations (COVID-19 Scenario).

For details of the data assumptions and calculations, please see the methodological appendix.

Salaries and Staffing

Select one of two default salary levels. Selecting “Current Salaries” will use salary data that reflects the current wages paid to the early childhood workforce. Selecting “Higher salaries” will use salary data that aligns more closely with public school salaries. See the methodology for full details of salary data used in the calculator. Users can also edit the salary data and enter new values in either the Annual Salary column or the Hourly Wage column.

On this tab the cost per child results generated on the Child Care Center and Family Child Care Home tabs are used to estimate the cost to fund (1) the monthly fixed costs of a specified percentage of the providers in the state and (2) the full monthly operating cost of a specified percentage of child care slots in the state. Data on the number of child care centers and family child care homes in the selected state, and the capacity of those programs, is drawn from CAP’s child care deserts database but can also be updated by the user.
